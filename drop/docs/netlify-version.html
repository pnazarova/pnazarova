About

A Specialist in Being a Generalist

Described by Stanford's Matt Abrahams as "truly a renaissance man," Gregory La Blanc teaches across more disciplines than virtually any other business school professor in the world.

With degrees in History, Philosophy, Politics & Economics, Business (Wharton), and Law, Greg has spent over two decades helping leaders navigate the intersection of technology, finance, strategy, and organizational change.

As a Distinguished Teaching Fellow at UC Berkeley's Haas School of Business, Lecturer at Stanford Graduate School of Business, and Faculty Director of the Berkeley Fintech Institute, he has worked with executives at over 100 companies and governments worldwide.

He is also the Academic Director of the AMENA Center for Entrepreneurship and Development, bridging Silicon Valley innovation with Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa.

“Curiosity is a natural human instinct, which is almost exterminated as you get older. I’ve just been fighting that specialization — fighting that urge to rinse, wash, repeat — for my entire life.”
