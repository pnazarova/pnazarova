About

Described by Stanford's Matt Abrahams as “truly a renaissance man,” Gregory La Blanc is a specialist in being a generalist — an interdisciplinary thinker who bridges technology, finance, law, and strategy.

As a Distinguished Teaching Fellow at UC Berkeley's Haas School of Business, Lecturer at Stanford Graduate School of Business, and Instructor at HEC Paris, Greg teaches across six schools at three elite universities — spanning more disciplines than virtually any other business school professor in the world.

He is the founder and Faculty Director of the Berkeley Fintech Institute, Academic Director of the AMENA Center for Entrepreneurship and Development, and host of the unSILOed podcast with over 619 episodes of interdisciplinary conversations.

With experience working alongside executives at over 100 companies and governments worldwide, Greg brings frameworks, tools, and perspectives that help leaders understand and navigate a world of accelerating technological change.
